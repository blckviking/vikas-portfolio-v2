How it works
01

Client submits brief via self-serve portal

A lightweight intake form replaces back-and-forth emails. The AI bot parses intent, product type, and requirements using an LLM — no human in the loop needed.

Old: 2–3 day CSM handoff New: instant parse <1s
02

MCP server routes & configures

The Model Context Protocol server acts as the orchestration layer — calling the right APIs (Cuelinks, Zingoy), provisioning accounts, and setting default KPI dashboards based on client type.

Automated account setup
03

Personalised welcome & activation flow

The bot generates a personalised onboarding sequence — contextual tips, first-action prompts, and integration docs — tailored to the client's platform (B2B publisher or B2C consumer).

Dynamic, not templated
04

KPIs tracked from day 0

Activation rate, engagement, and 30/60/90-day retention counters start from first interaction. No manual setup, no data lag. Defined and tracked via Mixpanel.

Metrics live from minute 1

An engineering problem
reframed as a product opportunity.

Most teams treat onboarding as a CS burden. I saw it as the product's first impression — and the biggest lever for activation. By owning the full spec from intake form to KPI dashboard, the team shipped something that scaled without adding headcount.

This was built with a small cross-functional squad: 2 engineers, 1 designer, and my product direction. We went from brief to production in 10 weeks.
