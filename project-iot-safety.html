The Challenge
Building a product where failure isn't an option — literally. Driver safety and fleet monitoring, at enterprise scale.

Safety Connect monitors driver behaviour — telematics, incident detection, compliance reporting — and fleet safety for organisations where a missed alert or a poor UX decision carries real-world consequences.

I owned the full product lifecycle across 32 enterprise clients and 50K+ monthly active users: roadmap, delivery, post-launch iteration, and retention. The platform had to be reliable, fast, and trusted.

The hard part wasn't shipping features — it was prioritising them. Every sprint balanced new enterprise demands against the stability that safety-critical clients depend on.

What I learned
Lesson 01
Safety-critical products demand a different prioritisation
In consumer products a bad UX is annoying. In safety IoT it can mean a missed alert on a real incident. I learned to triage ruthlessly — stability and reliability always above feature velocity.
Lesson 02
Enterprise clients are partners, not just users
Monthly steering meetings with 32 enterprise clients taught me the best decisions come from genuine partnership, not surveys. Co-designing features with clients produced the highest adoption.
Lesson 03
Market expansion needs a business case, not instinct
The Process Safety launch meant convincing leadership to invest in an adjacent market. I built a financial model, TAM analysis, and competitive landscape before a single line of code was written.
